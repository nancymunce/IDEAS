Estimating Time-on-Task for Online Courses

Use the estimates and tools below to set clear workload expectations that align with SPC credit hours and support student success.

Undergrad rule: ~3 hrs/week per credit Typical term: 15 weeks Online = online + offline work

Credit Hour & Time-on-Task

In face-to-face formats, “seat time” tracks contact minutes. In online learning, SPC emphasizes time-on-task—the total time students spend engaging with learning activities and assessments (reading, viewing, discussion, practice, collaboration, and reflection).

Design judgment matters. These figures are planning guides. Instructors and designers should calibrate workload by activity types, course level, prior knowledge, and modality.

Undergraduate Planning Estimates

Approximate weekly workload by credit and term length (online activities + independent work):

Undergraduate workload by credits and term length
Credits 15-Week Term 8-Week Term 3-Week Term
3~9 hrs/week~17 hrs/week~45 hrs/week
2~6 hrs/week~12 hrs/week~30 hrs/week
1~3 hrs/week~6 hrs/week~15 hrs/week

Example: a 3-credit, 15-week course totals about 135 hours (~9 hrs/week × 15 weeks).

Examples: 4-credit ≈225 total hours; 3-credit ≈180 total hours over 15 weeks.

Communicating Workload in Canvas

Suggested Syllabus Language

Copy/paste and adjust credits, weeks, and workload numbers:

“This course carries [X] credit hours over [Y] weeks. Students should plan for approximately [Z] hours per week completing readings, media, practice, discussions, and assessments. Actual time may vary based on prior knowledge and learning preferences.”
