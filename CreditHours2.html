Overview

The traditional credit hour stems from the Carnegie model (contact time plus outside preparation). For online learning, planning focuses on time on task—the overall time students spend engaging with course activities: reading, viewing, discussion, collaboration, practice, and assessment.

Rule of thumb (undergraduate): roughly 3 hours of work per credit each week across a typical 15-week term.
3 hrs/credit/week (UG) 15-week example term Online = online + offline tasks

Undergraduate Estimates

Approximate weekly workload by course credits and term length:

Undergraduate workload by credits and term length
Credits 15-Week Term 8-Week Term 3-Week Term
3~9 hrs/week~17 hrs/week~45 hrs/week
2~6 hrs/week~12 hrs/week~30 hrs/week
1~3 hrs/week~6 hrs/week~15 hrs/week

Example: a 3-credit course over 15 weeks ≈ 135 total hours (~9 per week × 15 weeks).

Graduate Estimates

Graduate courses generally expect more time per week. Examples:

Graduate workload by credits and term length
Credits 15-Week Term 8-Week Term 3-Week Term
4~15 hrs/week~28 hrs/weekintensive/varies
3~12 hrs/week~24 hrs/weekintensive/varies
2~9 hrs/week~17 hrs/week~45 hrs/week
1~6 hrs/week~12 hrs/week~30 hrs/week

Examples: 4-credit ≈ 225 total hours; 3-credit ≈ 180 total hours across 15 weeks.
