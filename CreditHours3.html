Understanding the Credit Hour Standard

The classical **Carnegie definition** of a semester credit hour stipulates that one semester credit hour be awarded for fifteen sessions of 50-minutes duration in classroom lecture-recitation each requiring **two hours of outside preparation** by the student.

In online instruction, rather than focusing on "contact hours," an estimate of **Time on Task** is the necessary adaptation. This involves calculating the total time a student is expected to spend on all course activities, both online and offline.

These activities should include viewing, reading, writing, collaborations, and interactions.

Undergraduate Time on Task Estimation

The standard formula for an undergraduate course is: **For every 1 credit, the student has 3 total hours of work each week** for the full semester (typically 15 weeks).

For a full semester 3-credit course, an online undergraduate student should expect 9 hours of work per week for 15 weeks, totaling **135 hours** for the course.

Graduate Time on Task Estimation

Graduate students are expected to spend **more time** on their online course activities. Below is an estimation of *approximate* numbers of hours of work per week required:

  • A 4-credit 15-week graduate online course = **225 total hours**.
  • A 3-credit 15-week graduate online course = **180 total hours**.

Although we can provide these basic guidelines, determining how much time the work in a course would take the students each week is ultimately up to the course designer/subject matter expert.
