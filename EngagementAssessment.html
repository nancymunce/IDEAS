Scenario: The "Weekly Update" Assignment

Students are required to write a 400-word summary of the assigned reading (a chapter on formal business report writing). The submission is due Friday. The instructor provides general feedback on grammar and completeness the following Monday. Students are told the skill is important for their future careers.

Your Self-Check Challenge:

Consider: What dimension(s) of engagement are weak? What traits are missing?

Analysis of the "Weekly Update"

  • Weak Dimension: Cognitive Engagement

    Flaw: Requiring a simple "summary" only asks for recall/memorization (low-level thinking). It does not require analysis, application, or critical justification. (Missing Challenge)

  • Weak Trait: Interaction (Learner-to-Content)

    Flaw: The activity focuses purely on consumption and regurgitation. It fails to require students to interact with the concepts by applying them to a problem, scenario, or creating an artifact. (Missing meaningful Interaction)

  • Missing Trait: Feedback (Timeliness & Specificity)

    Flaw: Waiting until Monday (a multi-day gap) and only providing "general" feedback on grammar means the feedback is not timely or useful for improvement on the core skill (report writing).

Suggested Engagement Improvement:

Instead of a summary, require students to identify a common business communication mistake related to report writing from an external source and propose a solution using three concepts from the chapter. This demands Cognitive Engagement, increases Relevance, and promotes deeper Learner-to-Content interaction.

Key Takeaway: Design engagement into your activities by requiring participation, inviting student voice, and demanding deeper thinking. Use relevance, autonomy, interaction, challenge, and feedback to create learning experiences where students are actively involved, not passive observers.
