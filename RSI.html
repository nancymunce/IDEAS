Regular & Substantive Interaction (RSI)

Your Guide to Engagement and Compliance in Online Education

üßê What is RSI?

RSI is a federal requirement for distance education programs to be eligible for Title IV funding. (34 CFR 602.17(g))

It's more than just posting announcements or grading assignments. It's about creating a dynamic and engaging learning environment where instructors actively participate in the learning process and provide meaningful feedback and support to students.

Interaction occurs frequently and predictably throughout the course. This establishes a consistent instructor presence and helps students feel connected to the course.
Interaction is meaningful and relevant to the course content. It goes beyond simple administrative tasks and focuses on intellectual engagement and the exchange of ideas.
The instructor takes the lead in starting interaction, rather than solely relying on student-initiated contact.
The interaction is primarily centered on teaching and learning, providing guidance, feedback, and support to students.

üéØ Why Prioritize RSI?

RSI is crucial for outcomes, quality, and compliance.

  • Student Success: Improves student engagement, motivation, and learning outcomes in online courses.
  • Quality Assurance: Helps ensure that online courses meet quality standards and provide a comparable learning experience to face-to-face courses.
  • Accreditation Compliance: Meeting the RSI requirement is essential for maintaining accreditation and eligibility for federal funding.
  • Building Community: Fosters a sense of community and belonging, which can help reduce feelings of isolation and improve student retention.

üõ†Ô∏è Practical Strategies: Implementing RSI

Expand the sections below for actionable ideas you can implement in your online course design right away!

  • Welcome Announcement: Start the course with a welcoming announcement, overview, and clear expectations for interaction.
  • Course Updates: Send regular updates, reminders, and insights related to the course content.
  • Check-in Messages: Send personalized check-in messages to students who may be struggling or falling behind.
  • Interactive Lectures: Incorporate polls, quizzes, and discussion prompts into your lectures.
  • Discussion Forums: Facilitate engaging discussion forums where students can share ideas and ask questions.
  • Collaborative Projects: Design projects that require students to work together and interact with each other.
  • Multimedia Content: Use videos, podcasts, and infographics to enhance engagement.
  • Timely Feedback: Provide prompt feedback on assignments so students can learn from mistakes.
  • Personalized Feedback: Offer specific suggestions for improvement tailored to the individual student.
  • Audio/Video Feedback: Consider using recorded feedback for a more personal experience.
  • Rubrics: Use rubrics to provide clear expectations and consistent feedback.
  • Active Participation in Discussions: Actively participate in discussion forums by asking questions and sharing your own insights.
  • Office Hours: Offer virtual office hours where students can meet with you.
  • Q&A Forums: Create a dedicated forum for questions with timely responses from you or peers.
  • Personal Introductions: Share a video or biography to help students get to know you better.

‚ö†Ô∏è Addressing Common Concerns

Here are quick tips for managing common RSI challenges:

Time Commitment

Prioritize activities that will have the greatest impact on student learning and engagement. Implementing RSI is an investment in student success.

Scalability

For larger class sizes, use technology to automate some tasks and focus your time on providing meaningful, personalized feedback and support.

Student Participation

Not all students will actively participate in online discussions or activities. Use a variety of strategies to encourage participation, such as assigning participation grades or offering extra credit.

Regular and substantive interaction is a critical component of quality online education. By prioritizing RSI, you ensure a valuable and rewarding learning experience for your students.
