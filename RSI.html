RSI Compliance Framework: Regular and Substantive Interaction

A Technical and Pedagogical Guide for Distance Education Excellence

The Regulatory Foundation: Distance vs. Correspondence Education

Regular and Substantive Interaction (RSI) is the explicit federal demarcation point, codified under 34 CFR 602.17(g), separating Title IV eligible distance education from correspondence education. The necessity for this distinction arose from historical concerns regarding financial aid fraud and the need to ensure academic quality comparable to face-to-face instruction.

Compliance Imperative: Institutions offering more than 50% of their total course offerings via correspondence, or enrolling more than 50% of their students in such courses, are ineligible for Title IV financial aid programs. Correspondence programs are defined by interaction that is "limited, is not regular and substantive, and is primarily initiated by the student".

Impact of the 2021 Final Rule

The 2021 Final Rule revisions provided crucial clarity, defining the specific instructional activities required for compliance. This update necessitates that all distance learning courses explicitly document a strategy for RSI, including verifying that the instructor meets the "qualifications for instruction established by the institution’s accrediting agency". This regulatory specificity moves RSI implementation beyond generalized best practices into a mandatory, auditable course design requirement.

1. Substantive Interaction (The Academic Core) +

Substantive interaction must be meaningful, relevant to the course content, and focused on intellectual engagement and the exchange of ideas. Procedural tasks, like simple reminders or non-personalized grade postings, are insufficient.

The 2021 mandate requires documented evidence of at least two of the following five activities:

  • Providing Direct Instruction: Delivering contextualized, instructor-created content (e.g., video lectures addressing common errors).
  • Assessing or Providing Feedback on a Student’s Coursework: Detailed, personalized feedback going beyond numerical scores.
  • Providing Information or Responding to Questions about the Content of a Course: Active management of Q&A forums or scheduled content-focused support.
  • Facilitating a Group Discussion: Instructor participation to guide, synthesize, and correct academic discussion threads.
  • Other Instructional Activities: Activities approved by the institutional accreditor.
2. Regular Interaction (Predictability and Proactivity) +

Regular interaction requires consistent presence throughout the course and is enforced through two mandatory components :

  • Scheduled and Predictable Opportunity: Interactions must occur frequently and consistently. Course design must explicitly state the RSI plan, guaranteeing interaction commensurate with the course length (e.g., weekly discussion participation or office hours).
  • Monitoring and Proactive Engagement: The instructor must monitor student academic success (e.g., submission rates, quiz scores). Based on this monitoring, the instructor must be responsible for promptly and proactively engaging in substantive interaction when a need is identified. Personalized check-in messages to struggling students are non-negotiable compliance evidence.
3. Instructor-Initiated Interaction (Non-Correspondence Differentiator) +

The instructor must take the lead in directing interaction; reliance on the student to initiate contact is the core definition of non-compliant correspondence education. Proactive communication, mandatory discussion facilitation, and targeted check-ins are all critical strategies that fulfill this pillar. Course design elements must ensure instructor initiation is woven into the required workflow, making interaction a mandatory component of the educational framework.

4. Focus on Instruction +

Interaction must be centered primarily on teaching and learning, providing academic guidance, feedback, and support related directly to the course content or competency. Administrative tasks—while necessary—do not count toward RSI compliance unless paired with substantive academic commentary. The goal is intellectual engagement, not task management.

ID Blueprint: Operational Strategies for RSI Compliance

Effective instructional design must translate regulatory requirements into concrete pedagogical actions that generate auditable evidence of RSI.

Proactive Communication and Presence

Faculty must maintain a consistent and visible presence. This begins with a personal welcome message or video, setting clear expectations for communication policies, including faculty response times.[14, 15] Throughout the course, faculty should use weekly updates not just for logistical reminders but to connect ideas, address trends in student submissions, or highlight key concepts, transforming procedural announcements into substantive instruction. Furthermore, compliance demands a system for proactive outreach; personalized check-in messages must be sent to students flagged by LMS monitoring as potentially struggling (e.g., non-submission alerts).

Designing for Meaningful Feedback

Providing "meaningful feedback" is a core Substantive activity. Feedback must be timely to allow students to apply improvements to subsequent work, facilitating a learning cycle. Personalized feedback addresses specific student needs and provides clear suggestions for improvement, moving beyond simple letter grades. Utilizing multimedia options, such as audio or video feedback on assignments, further enhances the instructor's personal presence and strengthens the sense of Substantive engagement. Transparency is maintained through the use of detailed rubrics that articulate clear expectations for consistent assessment.

Instructor Presence in Academic Activities

Compliant course activities require documented instructor involvement. For instance, in a History course, the instructor might facilitate an online debate using primary source documents and provide feedback on research papers. In English, this could mean virtual writing workshops alongside personalized essay feedback. The key is the instructor's active role: providing detailed solutions to challenging Mathematics problems in a Q&A session, or guiding the analysis of lab reports in Science. These actions provide the necessary evidence of direct instruction, assessment, and facilitation required under the Substantive pillar.
