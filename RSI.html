Regular & Substantive Interaction (RSI)

Your Guide to Engagement and Compliance in Online Education

üßê What is RSI?

[cite_start]RSI is a federal requirement (34 CFR 602.17(g)) for distance education programs to be eligible for **Title IV funding**[cite: 7].

It is more than just posting announcements or grading. [cite_start]It's about creating a **dynamic and engaging learning environment** where instructors actively participate and provide meaningful feedback and support[cite: 8].

---

üåü The Four Pillars: Key Components of RSI

Click or tap on the cards below to reveal the crucial characteristics of each pillar.

Regular
**Interaction must occur frequently and predictably** throughout the course. [cite_start]This builds a consistent presence and connection for students[cite: 10, 20].
Substantive
[cite_start]Interaction is **meaningful, relevant to the content**, and focuses on intellectual engagement and the exchange of ideas‚Äîgoing beyond administrative tasks[cite: 11, 12, 19].
Instructor-Initiated
[cite_start]The **instructor takes the lead** in starting interaction, not solely relying on students to make the first contact[cite: 13, 23].
Focus on Instruction
[cite_start]The interaction is primarily centered on **teaching and learning**, providing guidance, feedback, and support to students[cite: 14, 24].

üéØ Why Prioritize RSI?

RSI is crucial for outcomes, quality, and compliance.

    [cite_start]
  • Student Success: Improves engagement, motivation, and learning outcomes[cite: 29].
    • [cite_start]
  • Quality Assurance: Ensures online courses meet quality standards comparable to face-to-face[cite: 30].
    • [cite_start]
  • Accreditation/Funding: Essential for maintaining accreditation and eligibility for federal funding (Title IV)[cite: 31, 7].
    • [cite_start]
  • Building Community: Fosters a sense of belonging, reduces isolation, and improves student retention[cite: 32, 74].
