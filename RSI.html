Regular & Substantive Interaction (RSI)

Your Guide to Engagement and Compliance in Online Education

üßê What is RSI?

[cite_start]RSI is a federal requirement (34 CFR 602.17(g)) for distance education programs to be eligible for Title IV funding[cite: 7].

It is more than just posting announcements or grading. [cite_start]It's about creating a dynamic and engaging learning environment where instructors actively participate and provide meaningful feedback and support to students[cite: 8].

üåü The Four Pillars: Key Components of RSI

Click or tap on the cards below to reveal the crucial characteristics of each pillar.

Regular
[cite_start]Interaction must occur frequently and predictably throughout the course[cite: 10, 20]. [cite_start]This builds a consistent presence and connection for students[cite: 10].
Substantive
[cite_start]Interaction is meaningful, relevant to the content [cite: 11, 18, 19][cite_start], and focuses on intellectual engagement and the exchange of ideas‚Äîgoing beyond simple administrative tasks[cite: 12].
Instructor-Initiated
[cite_start]The instructor takes the lead in starting interaction [cite: 13, 23][cite_start], rather than solely relying on student-initiated contact[cite: 13].
Focus on Instruction
[cite_start]The interaction is primarily centered on teaching and learning [cite: 14, 24][cite_start], providing guidance, feedback, and support to students[cite: 14].

üéØ Why Prioritize RSI?

[cite_start]

RSI is crucial for outcomes, quality, and compliance[cite: 28].

    [cite_start]
  • Student Success: Improves student engagement, motivation, and learning outcomes in online courses[cite: 29].
    • [cite_start]
  • Quality Assurance: Helps ensure online courses meet quality standards and provide a comparable learning experience to face-to-face courses[cite: 30].
    • [cite_start]
  • Accreditation/Funding: Meeting the RSI requirement is essential for maintaining accreditation and eligibility for federal funding[cite: 31].
    • [cite_start]
  • Building Community: Fosters a sense of community and belonging, which can help reduce feelings of isolation and improve student retention[cite: 32].
